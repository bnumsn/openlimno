# Horizon Europe Application Outline

> Target call: **HORIZON-CL6-2026-BIODIV-01** (Cluster 6 Food, Bioeconomy,
> Natural Resources, Agriculture and Environment) — Biodiversity and
> ecosystem services destination, with a co-target on
> **HORIZON-INFRA-2026-DEV-01** (Research infrastructures developments).

## Why Horizon Europe

- WFD compliance and Restoration Law (2024 EU Nature Restoration
  Regulation) require quantitative ecological status assessments for
  every Member State by 2030.
- WFD-CIS Common Implementation Strategy explicitly calls for
  open-source tools to ensure cross-border reproducibility.
- HE consortia commonly include US/CN affiliated members as Associated
  Partners — perfect fit for our tri-jurisdictional framing.

## Project acronym

**OPENLIMNO-EU** — *Open Infrastructure for Instream-Flow and
Ecological Status Assessment under the EU Water Framework Directive*

## EU pilot rivers (WP6, candidate selection)

- **Czech Republic**: Vltava tributary (Czech BWS network)
- **Italy**: Po tributary or Tiber (CNR-IRSA monitoring stations)
- **Germany**: Elbe or Rhine reach (BfG monitoring stations)

Each pilot must produce:
- WFD ecological status report (EQR + classification)
- Cross-validation against existing Member-State methodology
- Reviewer-of-record sign-off (one EU-WFD expert per case)

## Impact framework (HE-mandatory)

### Scientific impact
- ≥ 12 peer-reviewed papers (Open Access mandatory under HE)
- One position paper in *Science of the Total Environment* on
  cross-jurisdictional reproducibility
- Active contribution to the WFD-CIS Common Implementation Strategy

### Socio-economic impact
- Reduction in WFD assessment cost per water body (target: 30%
  reduction vs. commercial-software baseline by Year 3)
- ≥ 100 EU water bodies assessed using OpenLimno by end of project
- Open data: all case-study datasets archived on Zenodo / B2SHARE

### Policy impact
- Direct input to WFD-CIS workgroup on hydromorphology
- Reference implementation listed in the next WFD-CIS guidance update
  (post-2028)
- Consortium member representation on JRC ECOSTAT working group

## Open Science compliance (HE-mandatory)

- Code: Apache-2.0 (already)
- Data: CC-BY-4.0, archived on Zenodo + EOSC
- Plan S compliance for all peer-reviewed outputs (Open Access
  mandatory; APCs included in budget)
- DMP filed within 6 months per HE Annotated Model Grant Agreement
- FAIR data principles applied to WEDM (the data model is already F+I+R;
  Accessibility ensured by EOSC archiving)

## Excellence (HE evaluation criterion 1, ≥ 4.0/5.0 target)

- Tri-jurisdictional framing (US/CN/EU) is unique in the EU funding
  landscape — most water-resource projects are EU-only
- Apache-2.0 license + CLA (DCO sign-off) means immediate uptake by
  industry partners (DHI, BMT, RAMSAR-network groups)
- Bovee 1997 closed-form regression ≤1e-3 already demonstrated —
  technical risk is low
